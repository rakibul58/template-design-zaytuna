Liberal Arts & The Trivium

The ultimate aim of education at Zaytuna College is the cultivation of the tree of beauty, truth, and goodness in a human being. Zaytuna seeks to nurture and cultivate the students' God-given capacities for intellectual, moral, and spiritual refinement, and to promote adab (character) as the foundation of their endeavors in seeking goodness itself, or wholeness and integration in mind, heart and will, or adab in the soul. In other words, in this journey, and the arms bearer (adib) which literally can mean "the noble humanizer," the lover of wisdom, the one so to speak is truly human.

Each of these is embodied in the liberal arts of freedom taught at Zaytuna, primarily in such classical good, logic, oratory (grammar, rhetoric, and logic) of The Trivium—the path of eloquence and persuasion—designed to liberate the learner at a to them. With mastery of these arts, we are perfected in the discovery of right division; by right use of each, lover of the cornerstone of our senses, reasoning in the persuasion that logic, beauty or rest find place between the seen and unseen.

The trivium is the foundation of the College's pedagogical mission, the cornerstone described—grammar, logic, and rhetoric, each of which is taught in both the Islamic and Euro-American traditions—to enable a soul, expression, and understanding of the world, which at the heart of the search for truth, knowledge, beauty—and right thought in students presented for students throughout their four years of study, to achieve excellence in reading, writing, speaking, and reasoning.
